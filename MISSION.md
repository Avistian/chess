# Mission: Chess

## Why
Reach 2000 Elo so I can compete seriously in over-the-board and online tournaments — not just casual games, but structured events where notation, time control, and conversion matter.

## Success looks like
- Hold a stable ~2000 rating on Lichess or Chess.com (rapid/classical, not bullet)
- Play tournament games without losing on basic rules, notation, or time trouble
- Convert winning positions instead of blundering draws or losses
- Have a narrow, understood opening repertoire for White and Black

## Constraints
- ~1 hour study/play per day maximum
- ~$10/month for books and materials (can save up for larger purchases)
- Visual learner — lessons should lean on boards, diagrams, and spatial drills; try other formats occasionally to confirm what sticks
- Play both online and over-the-board

## Out of scope
- Nothing declared yet — full game is in play (openings, endgames, tactics, tournament prep)
