# Chess Resources

## Knowledge

- [Lichess Learn — interactive piece movement](https://lichess.org/learn)
  Free, no-account-required walkthrough of how each piece moves. Use for: absolute beginners who have never set up a board.
- [Lichess Practice — tactics and checkmates](https://lichess.org/practice)
  Structured drill sets from basic mates through pins, forks, and endgame theory. Use for: skill-building after the rules are known.
- [Lichess Training — daily puzzles](https://lichess.org/training)
  Free rated puzzle stream matched to your level. Use for: the daily 15-minute tactics block in the study roadmap.
- [GothamChess — How To Play Chess: The Ultimate Beginner Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OCSbzArwB10)
  IM Levy Rozman's 30-minute overview covering board setup, piece movement, checkmate, openings, tactics, endgames, and a study plan. Use for: a single high-quality video orientation to the whole game.
- [Chess Fundamentals — José Capablanca (public domain)](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/3387)
  Classic text by a world champion. Clear prose, minimal jargon. Use for: reading-based learners who want timeless fundamentals without platform lock-in.
- [Logical Chess: Move by Move — Irving Chernev](https://www.amazon.com/Logical-Chess-Move-Irving-Chernev/dp/0713484640)
  33 annotated games explaining every single move in plain language. Use for: understanding *why* moves are played, not just what the rules allow.
- [Silman's Complete Endgame Course — Jeremy Silman](https://www.amazon.com/Silmans-Complete-Endgame-Course-Chess/dp/1904600012)
  Endgame material organized by rating band — study only what matches your level. Use for: converting winning positions instead of blundering draws.
- [Chessable — spaced-repetition courses](https://www.chessable.com/)
  Free and paid courses with built-in review scheduling. Use for: memorizing opening lines or drilling tactical patterns with retention.
- [Old School Chess — Fastest Path to 2000](https://oldschoolchess.com/learn/guides/fastest-path-to-2000)
  Phased study plan (tactics-heavy foundation → strategy → refinement). Use for: calibrating weekly time split at each rating band.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/chessbeginners](https://reddit.com/r/chessbeginners)
  Welcoming subreddit for new players. Use for: rule questions, "what should I study next?" advice, sharing first wins.
- [Lichess Forum](https://lichess.org/forum)
  Active community tied to the free platform. Use for: study advice, finding sparring partners, opening questions.
- [Chess.com Forums](https://www.chess.com/forum)
  Large community with daily activity. Use for: casual discussion, game review requests, tournament talk.
- [Chess Stack Exchange](https://chess.stackexchange.com/)
  Q&A with strong moderation. Use for: precise rules questions and study-plan debates.
