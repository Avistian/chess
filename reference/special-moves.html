Special Moves Cheat Sheet

Quick reference · tournament rules

Castling

NotationMeaning
O-OKingside — king to g-file, rook to f-file (White)
O-O-OQueenside — king to c-file, rook to d-file (White)

Illegal when: king in check; king crosses attacked square; king or rook already moved; pieces between them.

Legal even when: the rook is attacked (only the king's path must be safe).

En passant

  • Enemy pawn moves two squares from its start and lands beside your pawn
  • You may capture on the square it skipped
  • Must be played on the next move only
  • Notation: exd6 (file of capturing pawn + × + destination square)

Promotion

  • Pawn reaches the 8th rank (1st for Black)
  • Becomes Q, R, B, or N — your choice
  • Notation: e8=Q (optional; =Q often implied)

Tournament habits

  • Touch-move: touch a piece you can move → you must move it
  • Adjust: say "j'adoube" (or "adjust") before centering a piece without moving it
  • Write moves in algebraic notation on every serious game
