Basic Tactics Cheat Sheet

Quick reference · forks, pins, loose pieces

Scan order (beginner → ~1400)

  1. Loose pieces — undefended enemy pieces; take them.
  2. Checks that fork — especially knight forks on king + queen/rook.
  3. Pins — pieces frozen on a line to the king or a valuable piece behind.

Fork

  • One move attacks two or more enemy pieces.
  • Defender saves one; you win the other.
  • Knights fork most often — they jump and ignore blockers.
  • Classic: Nf7 hitting king on e8 and queen on d8.

Pin

  • Piece on a line cannot move without exposing something behind it.
  • Absolute pin to the king — moving is illegal (king would be in check).
  • Relative pin to queen/rook — moving is legal but usually losing.
  • Bishops, rooks, and queens create pins along diagonals, files, and ranks.

Loose piece (hanging / undefended)

  • Enemy piece with zero defenders on its square.
  • Often the whole tactic — no combination required.
  • After every opponent move: what did they leave undefended?
