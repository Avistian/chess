Chess Glossary

Quick reference · updated as lessons progress

Algebraic notation
Standard system for recording moves. Squares named by file + rank (e.g. e4). Pieces by letter: K, Q, R, B, N.
File
Vertical column of squares. Labeled a–h from White's left.
Rank
Horizontal row of squares. Labeled 1–8 from White's side.
Check
The king is under attack and must escape on the next move.
Checkmate
The king is in check and has no legal escape. Game over.
Stalemate
Side to move has no legal move but is not in check. Game is a draw.
Castling
One-time king move: king slides two squares toward a rook; rook jumps to the other side. Kingside (O-O) or queenside (O-O-O).
En passant
Special pawn capture when an opponent pawn advances two squares and lands beside your pawn.
Elo / Rating
Numeric estimate of playing strength. 2000 ≈ strong club / expert level on most online platforms.
Tactic
Short combination exploiting a concrete opportunity — fork, pin, skewer, discovered attack, etc.
Opening repertoire
The set of openings you play regularly as White and Black. At your level, narrow and understood beats wide and memorized.
