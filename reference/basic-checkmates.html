Basic Checkmates Cheat Sheet

Quick reference · queen & rook vs lone king

Core idea

Shrink the box — reduce the squares the enemy king can use until it is on the edge or in a corner, then mate. Restrict before you chase with checks.

Queen + king vs king

  • Queen stays a knight-move away from the enemy king while boxing (not adjacent on the edge).
  • Each queen move cuts off a file, rank, or diagonal band of escape squares.
  • Your king walks in after the box is small — keep kings at least two squares apart.
  • Watch for stalemate: if the king has no legal move and is not in check, the game is a draw.

Rook + king vs king (ladder)

  • Rook cuts off one file or rank (e.g. Ra8 traps king on the 8th rank).
  • Push the king toward the edge with rook checks or quiet rook slides.
  • Your king follows on the same file/rank until the rook delivers mate on the back rank or a-file.
  • Two rooks vs king: alternate checks along ranks — faster, same principle.

Before you play the “winning” move

QuestionIf yes…
Is the enemy king in check?Good — but not necessarily mate.
Does the enemy king have a legal square?Not mate yet — keep boxing.
Would the enemy have no legal move and not be in check?Stalemate — do not play that move.
