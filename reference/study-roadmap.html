Study Roadmap to 2000

Reference · ~1 hour/day · tournament focus

2000 Elo from a rusty beginner is a multi-year project with consistent daily work — not a sprint. Most adults reach it in 3–5 years at 45–90 min/day (Old School Chess). Your 1-hour cap is enough if every minute is deliberate.

Three phases

PhaseRating bandFocus
1 · Foundation Beginner → ~1400 Rules fluency, coordinates, basic mates, 70% tactics, elementary endgames
2 · Growth ~1400 → ~1700 50% tactics, 25% strategy, 25% endgames; narrow opening repertoire
3 · Refinement ~1700 → 2000 Balanced study; deep game analysis; conversion; clock management

Daily hour (once foundation is set)

  1. 15 min — Tactics puzzles (Lichess Training or Chess.com puzzles)
  2. 20 min — Current study topic (endgame chapter, strategy, or opening plans)
  3. 25 min — One slow game (15+ min) or manual analysis of your last loss — no engine first

Book budget (~$10/mo)

Suggested purchase order — save 2 months per book:

  1. Logical Chess: Move by Move — Chernev (~$15) · why moves are played
  2. Silman's Complete Endgame Course (~$16) · conversion at every level
  3. 1001 Chess Exercises for Beginners (~$12) · tactics workbook

Until then: Lichess Practice and puzzles are free and sufficient for Phase 1.

Tournament habits to build early

  • Write moves in algebraic notation on every serious game
  • Play with a clock — even online rapid (15+10) before bullet
  • Analyze losses before wins
  • Hold one training priority for 6–10 weeks before switching
